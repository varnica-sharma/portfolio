Benchmarking Machine Learning Techniques in Under-Resourced Contexts

Analysis of Public Perceptions of Government Policies from South Karnataka Reddit Discourse

6th International Conference on Communications, Information, Electronic and Energy Systems November 26-28, 2025 â€¢ Ruse, Bulgaria

Abstract: Understanding public perception is crucial for successful policy implementation. This study employs established Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to gauge public sentiment on government policies in the South Karnataka region of India. We conducted a comprehensive comparative analysis, benchmarking five traditional machine learning models (Support Vector Machine, Decision Tree, NaÃ¯ve Bayes, K-Nearest Neighbors, and Random Forest) against the deep learning model DistilBERT under challenging low-data conditions typical of under-resourced regional contexts.

Our findings reveal that SVM achieved 41.5% accuracy, slightly outperforming DistilBERT (40.4%), highlighting the importance of domain-specific validation even for powerful pre-trained models. For thematic analysis using unsupervised clustering, K-Means demonstrated exceptional performance with 94.6% cluster coherence, producing highly separable, keyword-driven topics compared to BERTopic's more semantically nuanced but overlapping clusters.

This research establishes the first performance baseline for sentiment analysis and topic modeling of public discourse on health policies within regional Indian social media contexts, providing critical insights for policymakers and NLP practitioners working with limited-resource datasets.
